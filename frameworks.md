# Core Theoretical Frameworks

This section presents the three primary conceptual frameworks developed in this research archive.

## Consciousness Field Hypothesis (CFH)

**Core Concept:** Exploring consciousness as a fundamental field in the universe, analogous to electromagnetic or gravitational fields.

**Key Elements:**
- Participatory universe model where observers shape reality
- Field coherence effects enabling non-local correlations
- Observer-architect principle for reality construction
- Mathematical formalization of consciousness field dynamics

**Research Materials:**
- CFH_Participatory_Framework_Main.pdf
- CFH_Physics_Participation_Coherence.pdf  
- CFH_Amplification_Model_Core.pdf

## Quantum Consciousness Amplification (QCA)

**Core Concept:** Investigating whether quantum field effects could theoretically amplify consciousness states and enhance cognitive capabilities.

**Key Elements:**
- Scalar field amplification mechanisms
- Neural interface technologies
- Quantum coherence in biological systems
- Consciousness enhancement protocols

**Research Materials:**
- QCA_Main_Document_v1.pdf
- QCA_Scalar_Field_Model_Neural.pdf
- QCA_Complete_Theory.pdf

## Hyperchronal Quantum Theory (HQT)

**Core Concept:** Examining time as recursive depth rather than linear progression, with implications for consciousness and causality.

**Key Elements:**
- Non-linear temporal structures
- Retrocausal information flows
- Ontological state management
- Hyperchronal loop dynamics

**Research Materials:**
- HQT_Hyperchronal_Ontology_Inversion.pdf
- HQT_Time_Recursive_Depth_Original.pdf
